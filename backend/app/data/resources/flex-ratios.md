# Flex ratio reference

Flex ratio = **seats / FTE**. A ratio of 0.75 = 7.5 seats per 10 people
(2.5 expected off-site on peak day). The right ratio depends on
presence policy, work typology, team shape, and the infrastructure
that supports flexibility.

---

## 1. Definitions and conventions

- **Assigned seat** — one seat belongs to one named person.
- **Neighbourhood flex** — seats assigned to a team zone ; any member
  of the team takes any seat within that zone on arrival.
- **Fully unassigned (activity-based)** — any seat, any day ; bookable
  via an app.
- **Flex ratio / desk-sharing ratio** — seats per FTE ; values < 1.0
  mean fewer seats than people.
- **Utilisation rate** — fraction of seats occupied at any instant (or
  averaged over the working day).
- **Occupancy rate** — fraction of seats allocated to active users (an
  org can over-allocate beyond 100 % in a flex regime).

## 4. Desk-sharing — current industry state

Sources : FlexOS 2024 analysis, Ronspot 2026 workplace statistics
report, CBRE Hybrid Reality brief, OfficeRnD 2024, JLL Global Research,
Leesman 2024.

### 4.1 Headline numbers (2024 – 2025)

- **69 %** of real-estate client organisations have **> 40 %** of
  their population sharing desks (FlexOS 2024)
- **48 %** of organisations target a sharing ratio between **1.01 and
  1.49 pers/seat** in 2025, up from 21 % in 2024 — a doubling in one
  year
- Share ratios **≥ 1.5 pers/seat** have grown **+ 93 %** since 2023
- Dedicated desk share : **51 % (2021) → 40 % (2024)**
- Office occupancy rate (allocation) : **101 % (2024) → 111 % (2025)**
  — over-allocation is now the norm in flex regimes
- Office **utilisation** rate : **41 % (2023) → 49 % (2024) → 54 %
  (2025)** ; pre-pandemic baseline was 61 %
- Only **22 %** of employees work full-time in an office, while **67 %**
  of firms offer location flexibility

### 4.2 Utilisation peak profile

Peak-day occupancy typically **1.20 – 1.35 × average** on Tuesday /
Wednesday / Thursday. A flex plant sized to the average breaks on peak
days ; size to the peak and accept under-utilisation Monday / Friday.

### 4.3 Sector pattern

- **Tech / software** : most aggressive flex adopters, ratios often
  **0.5 – 0.7**
- **Financial services** : more conservative, ratios **0.8 – 0.9** for
  trading floors, **0.7 – 0.8** for support
- **Legal / consulting** : senior partners frequently retain assigned
  seats, junior / associate cohorts at **0.5 – 0.65**
- **Pharma / R&D** : lab spaces assigned ; office / admin around
  **0.7 – 0.8**
- **Public sector** : slower adoption, **0.8 – 0.95**

## 5. Sizing a seat pool — methodology

### 5.1 Derive target ratio from presence policy

| Presence policy         | Avg. presence | Peak day factor | Target seats/FTE  |
|-------------------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 5/0 (full on-site)      | ~95 %         | 1.00            | **≥ 1.0**         |
| 4/1                     | ~80 %         | 1.15            | **0.90 – 0.95**   |
| **3/2 (hybrid standard, Lumen)** | **~60 %** | **1.25**  | **0.70 – 0.80**   |
| 2/3                     | ~40 %         | 1.30            | **0.55 – 0.65**   |
| 1/4 or fully flexible   | ~20 – 30 %    | 1.40            | **0.40 – 0.55**   |

### 5.2 Peak-day model

`seats ≥ (FTE × average_presence × peak_day_factor)`

For Lumen at 170 FTE horizon with 3/2 policy :
- `170 × 0.60 × 1.25 = 128 seats minimum`
- Choose **130 – 136 seats** for slight headroom (maps to 0.76 – 0.80
  seats/FTE)

### 5.3 Neighbourhood sizing

If you assign pools to teams : **size each pool at 0.8 × peak team
size**, not at average team size. A 12-person team should get at
least 10 seats in its neighbourhood.

## 6. Lumen worked example

**Brief** : 120 FTE today → 170 FTE in 24 months, policy 3/2, culture
collaborative with strong team identity (produit / tech / data /
growth / ops), pair programming intensive.

- **Target ratio** : **0.75 seats/FTE** (middle of 0.70 – 0.80
  fourchette for 3/2 policy)
- **At 170 FTE horizon** :
  - 0.70 × 170 = **119 seats**
  - 0.75 × 170 = **128 seats**
  - 0.80 × 170 = **136 seats**
- **Peak day model at 0.75** : average 102 on-site, peak 128
  (1.25 ×) → pick **130 seats** (slight headroom) or **136 seats**
  (comfortable)
- **Neighbourhood split** (5 teams) :
  - Produit : 35 FTE × 0.75 = **26 seats**
  - Tech : 55 FTE × 0.75 = **41 seats** (pair programming → consider
    slightly higher ratio 0.80 = **44 seats**)
  - Data : 22 FTE × 0.75 = **17 seats**
  - Growth : 28 FTE × 0.75 = **21 seats**
  - Ops : 30 FTE × 0.75 = **23 seats**
  - Total : **128 – 131 seats** depending on tech adjustment

### 6.1 Saved real estate

Difference vs assigned-desk model :
- Assigned : **170 seats** × 11 m² NIA = 1 870 m²
- Flex 0.75 : **130 seats** × 11 m² = 1 430 m²
- **Freed surface : ~440 m²** — roughly 18 % of the 2 400 m² plate

This freed surface should **fund** collaboration, amenity, and
biophilic moves, not be claimed back as "saved rent". Lumen's brief
("cafétéria centrale forte", "beaucoup d'espaces collab") supports
this interpretation.

## 7. Three doctrines of unassigned / assigned

### 7.1 Assigned

- Every FTE has a named seat
- **When it fits** : 5/0 policy, confidential / regulated work, senior
  professional culture, high seniority expectations
- **Pro** : minimal friction, strong identity, predictable
- **Con** : worst real-estate efficiency ; no lever if policy shifts

### 7.2 Neighbourhood flex

- Seats assigned **to a team zone** (quartier) ; any member picks any
  seat within the zone
- **When it fits** : 3/2 or 4/1, strong team identity, product orgs
  (this is **Lumen's profile**)
- **Pro** : preserves identity, reduces seat count, maintains
  predictability
- **Con** : shared seats still need clean-desk discipline ; hot
  borrowing across teams discouraged

### 7.3 Fully unassigned (activity-based)

- No zone assignment, all seats bookable
- **When it fits** : 2/3 or lower presence, high mobility (sales,
  consulting, client-facing)
- **Pro** : maximum efficiency, encourages cross-team mixing
- **Con** : identity erodes unless strong ritual compensates ; onboarding
  harder ; locker and stuff-stash infrastructure critical

**Lumen verdict** : neighbourhood flex — their brief explicitly asks
for team identity, which fully unassigned would dilute.

## 8. Flex infrastructure requirements

Flex only works with the supporting infrastructure. Any of these
missing → anti-office backlash within 6 – 12 months.

### 8.1 Booking system

- Real-time display at each seat (Joan, Robin, Envoy, OfficeSpace,
  Office R&D) or QR-code check-in
- **Booking transparency** : free/booked state visible on app AND at
  the seat
- **Ghost-booking policy** : if a booked seat isn't checked in within
  30 min, release it

### 8.2 Locker infrastructure

- **One locker per FTE** minimum, ideally with asset-tagged RFID or
  PIN access
- Locker size : standard 400 × 400 × 400 mm cell ; larger (400 × 600
  × 400 mm) for coat/helmet/laptop-bag users
- Locker bank footprint : plan **0.3 – 0.5 m² per 10 lockers**
  including circulation

### 8.3 Concentration supply

- **≥ 6 phone booths / 100 FTE** (industry mid-range, higher for
  hybrid with heavy remote meetings)
- **≥ 4 focus rooms / 100 FTE** (small 1 – 2 person enclaves for 2 – 4 h
  head-down sessions)
- **≥ 3 – 5 huddle rooms / 100 FTE**

Removed seat headroom in flex is partly offset by **higher collab + focus
infrastructure demand** — budget accordingly.

### 8.4 Third-place infrastructure

Flex requires **"home for the day"** options :
- Café / lounge as working place (~15 – 25 % of programme)
- Outdoor terrace (weather-allowing)
- Phone booths for every 10 min call
- Social hubs (coffee points, water stations) no more than 20 m from
  any seat

### 8.5 Change-management ritual

- Clean-desk policy visibly enforced
- "Arrival ritual" : plug in, set up monitor, open locker ; 5 min max
- "Departure ritual" : wipe down, unplug, lock away personal items

## 9. Pitfalls to avoid

1. **Ratio too aggressive on day 1** — start at **0.80** and tighten to
   **0.70** after 12 – 18 months of utilisation data. Going straight
   to 0.60 without infra produces revolt.
2. **Neighbourhoods that don't match team shape** — a 12-person team
   cannot fit in a 10-seat zone on peak day. Size to **peak team at
   ratio**, not average at ratio.
3. **Ignoring peak day** — averaging across the week hides Tuesday
   pain.
4. **Removing dedicated desks without removing desk work** — if 40 %
   of the work is long-form solo focus, 0.6 ratio breaks.
5. **No stored personal items policy** — flex without lockers is
   resentment in two weeks.
6. **Partner / manager exceptions** — the day the C-suite keeps its
   private offices while requiring staff to flex, the policy is dead.

---

## 10. Forecast guidance

- Occupancy and utilisation metrics (54 % utilisation in 2025 vs 61 %
  pre-pandemic) suggest the ratio landing zone will **tighten further**
  over 2025 – 2027 — expect mainstream 0.65 – 0.75 for hybrid-standard
  organisations.
- Leesman H-Lmi gap of **10 points** confirms the office has to offer
  **something the home doesn't** — collaboration, serendipity, amenities,
  biophilia. Flex space freed by lower ratios should **primarily fund
  that premium**, not be clawed back as "saved real estate".
- AI adoption at scale is already reducing head-down work hours per
  FTE — ratios will likely loosen again if collaboration rebounds
  harder than individual focus ; continue measuring, don't commit to
  one ratio for 10 years.
