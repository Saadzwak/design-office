# design://client-profiles

Characteristic programming, spatial, and aesthetic signatures of the
industries Design Office targets. Used to bias the Brief programme, the
macro-zoning agent, the mood-board curator, and the argumentaire.

All ratios and figures come either from the other MCP resources
(`design://office-programming`, `design://flex-ratios`) or from the
industry benchmarks cited below. Anything not fully verified is marked
`[TO VERIFY]`.

---

## How to use this resource

Every generation in Design Office accepts a `client_profile` hint. It is
one of :

- `tech_startup`
- `law_firm`
- `bank_insurance`
- `consulting`
- `creative_agency`
- `healthcare`
- `public_sector`
- `other` (falls back to `tech_startup` defaults with explicit notes)

When an agent receives a profile, it should read the corresponding block
below and apply the **programming biases**, the **spatial biases**, and the
**material / tone biases**. Biases should be explicit in the output (e.g.
"Law-firm profile → closed-office ratio raised to 60%, citing Cushman
Wakefield 2023 Legal-Sector Workplace report [TO VERIFY]").

## 1. `tech_startup`

### Programming bias

- Flex ratio 0.6 – 0.8 (hybrid, 2-3 days on-site)
- Collab surface share high : 30-35 % of NIA
- Individual surface share medium : 28-34 %
- Phone booth count generous : ~1 per 8 FTE at peak day
- Large town hall + café central ; ritual space matters
- Open-plan team neighbourhoods with named districts
- Few fully enclosed offices ; leadership works from the floor

Sources : Gensler Workplace Survey 2024 (tech sector), Leesman 2019-2024
tech subset, Metcalf & co. _Open-plan in tech 2022_ [TO VERIFY].

### Spatial bias

- Workstations benefit from the daylight side (pair programming)
- Collab heart in the centre or against the quieter façade
- Quiet focus rings along a second façade
- Whiteboard-dense ; pinboards at every neighbourhood spine
- Cabling : high density, prefer raised floor or cable trays

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **warm minimal**. Light timber (oak, birch), white walls,
  pops of brand colour.
- Soft lighting 3000-3500 K in collab, 4000 K at desks.
- Plants densely placed in collab zones (Ficus Lyrata, Kentia,
  Monstera).
- Brand expression : strong but tasteful. Think Figma HQ, Stripe,
  Notion offices, Typeform Barcelona.
- Furniture references : Vitra Tyde / Hay Mags Soft / Muuto Outline /
  Wilkhahn ON / Framery One.

### Do not

- Mahogany, leather Chesterfields, heavy brass — reads as law-firm.
- Single-occupancy enclosed offices for mid-managers.
- Institutional beige carpets.

## 2. `law_firm`

### Programming bias

- Flex ratio **0.9 – 1.0** (associates and partners each want a desk)
- Individual surface share very high : **45-55 %** of NIA
- **30-60 % closed offices** (ratio depends on firm size, seniority
  pyramid) — not open-plan
- Meeting rooms : many, varied capacity, **confidential**
- Classic library / archive room still expected
- Client-facing area isolated from work zones (receptions, boardroom)
- Café / town hall smaller than tech — ~5 % of NIA

Sources : Cushman Wakefield _Legal Sector Workplace Trends_ 2023
[TO VERIFY], CBRE _Law Firm Outlook_ 2024 [TO VERIFY], Leesman legal
subset 2022-2024 [TO VERIFY].

### Spatial bias

- Offices line the façades (partners on the south / prime aspect)
- Open-plan or bench desks for paralegals / associates at the core
- Heavy acoustic separation between offices (DnT,A ≥ 40 dB target)
- Private meeting rooms close to the entrance for client intake
- Sound-masked circulation where confidentiality is at stake

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **wood + leather + dark green + brass**. High contrast,
  materials that age well.
- Lighting 3000 K in offices, 3500 K in corridors.
- Textured wallpaper or fabric panels, thick carpets, heavy curtains.
- Custom millwork for shelving (code books, archives).
- Furniture references : Walter Knoll, Andreu World Reverse, Herman
  Miller Eames Lobby, Vitra Grand Repos for client-facing lounge.
- Signature : dark library, deep bookcases, natural stone reception desk.

### Do not

- Open flex benches without fallback enclosed offices.
- Exposed ceilings / industrial aesthetic — reads as creative agency.
- Bright primary colours in client-facing areas.

## 3. `bank_insurance`

### Programming bias

- Flex ratio **0.8 – 0.95** (front office each has a desk, mid / back
  may share)
- Enclosed offices for senior management, flex for the rest
- Trading-room or client-advisor-room zones where relevant
- Strong segregation : client-facing / back-office / restricted zones
- Security-controlled circulations (badge zones) at ~5-10 % of floor
- Boardrooms : formal, multiple capacities, AV-rich
- Executive floor often separated architecturally

Sources : JLL _Financial Services Workplace 2024_ [TO VERIFY], CBRE
_Banking & Insurance Occupier Survey_ 2023 [TO VERIFY].

### Spatial bias

- Executive offices on the top floor or the best aspect
- Secure comms rooms, sometimes even discrete data zones on the fit-out
- Private banking lounges adjacent to reception but isolated
- More fully enclosed formal meeting rooms than the tech profile
- Reception : impressive, often double-height in flagship offices

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **neutrals + brass + stone + premium fabric**. Understated
  wealth, restraint, no gimmicks.
- Lighting 3000-3500 K throughout, warm accent at reception.
- Polished stone reception desk, textured wood panelling, brushed brass.
- High-end fabric upholstery (Kvadrat Remix, Maharam wool).
- Planting minimal but pristine — shaped topiaries, no messy vines.
- Furniture references : Poltrona Frau, Herman Miller Eames Aluminium,
  Walter Knoll Circo, Artemide Tolomeo.

### Do not

- Exposed concrete, painted OSB — reads as startup.
- Loud brand-coloured accents.
- Visibly casual furniture (beanbags, hammocks) in any zone.

## 4. `consulting`

### Programming bias

- Flex ratio **0.55 – 0.7** — consultants are on client sites most of
  the time; desks are hot and mobile
- Individual surface share low : 22-28 %
- Collab + meeting share very high : 40 % of NIA
- Client-facing meeting rooms numerous and varied
- Focus pods and phone booths at peak density (work-from-anywhere mode
  at HQ)
- Library / knowledge-base zone
- Café generous for pre-client huddles

Sources : Gensler _Consulting Workplace_ 2024 [TO VERIFY], Leesman
2024 professional-services cluster.

### Spatial bias

- Drop-in bench desks + focus pods at the core
- Huddles and presentation rooms along the client-facing spine
- Wayfinding extra clear (partners rotate offices often)
- Knowledge-base + training rooms in the back of house

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **clean modern, soft industrial**. Light grey, warm wood,
  black steel accents.
- Lighting 3500-4000 K, efficient.
- Signage-heavy, wayfinding strong, client lobby with digital art or
  data visualisations.
- Furniture references : Herman Miller Abak, USM Haller, Vitra AC5,
  Kinnarps Fields.

### Do not

- Too-fixed offices — consultants rarely use them.
- Residential warmth (sofas in every room) — dilutes the efficiency
  message.

## 5. `creative_agency`

### Programming bias

- Flex ratio 0.75 – 0.9 (creatives work on-site for flow)
- Project-room density high : every 8-12 FTE gets a bookable
  project room
- Material library + sampling + small production workshop
- Presentation / pitch rooms with high-spec AV
- Café / kitchen is **the** social hub, not reception
- Playful, identity-loud territory but functional

Sources : _AD_ studio tours 2022-2024 [TO VERIFY], case studies of
Bigbang, Ubiq, Saguez, MoreySmith fit-outs.

### Spatial bias

- Open flowing floorplate, few walls, reconfigurable furniture
- Gallery-style central spine for work-in-progress pinning
- Bench desks cluster around project-rooms
- Phone booths clustered near quiet focus
- Acoustic separation achieved with curtains, felt, textile baffles
  rather than hard partitions

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **bold, tonal + unexpected accent**. Plaster, raw
  plywood, coloured felt, terrazzo.
- Lighting mixed : 3000 K for moodboard review zones, 4000 K for
  production, theatrical accent lamps in lounge.
- Strong artworks / prints curated by the team.
- Furniture references : Vitra Softshell, Muuto Five, Magis Steelwood,
  Hay Copenhague.

### Do not

- Overly corporate meeting rooms — reads as consulting.
- Beige or institutional colours anywhere client-visible.

## 6. `healthcare`

### Programming bias

- Operates under healthcare + labour regulations (hygiene, ventilation
  minimums, ergonomics). Follow `design://pmr-requirements` +
  `design://ergonomic-workstation` strictly.
- Flex ratio **0.9 – 1.0** — clinicians rarely hot-desk
- Enclosed consult rooms, secure records storage
- Strict circulation widths (beds, trolleys): 1.80 m primary
- Sanitary zones with medical-grade sinks near consult rooms

Sources : HBN (Health Building Notes, UK) [TO VERIFY], _Planetree_
evidence-based healthcare design [TO VERIFY], NHS _Improving Working
Environment_ 2023 [TO VERIFY].

### Spatial bias

- Staff breakout zones separated from patient circulation
- Quiet rooms for writing / dictation next to consult rooms
- Calm, daylit areas for rest — biophilic cues supported by Ulrich
  (`design://neuroarchitecture`)
- Strong wayfinding (patients under stress)

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **warm neutrals + calm accents**. Muted greens, sky-blues,
  wood tones, nothing saturated.
- Lighting 3500 K, excellent CRI ≥ 90 in consult, 4000 K in back-office.
- Washable surfaces, antibacterial fabrics, rounded corners.
- Biophilic cues to offset stress : framed views, plants (non-allergenic
  species only).
- Furniture references : Herman Miller Compass, Steelcase Vital Reach,
  Wilkhahn Stand-up.

### Do not

- Open-plan clinical consult spaces (confidentiality breach).
- Cold institutional palette (overused beige + blue).
- Hard reflective surfaces everywhere (acoustic issue).

## 7. `public_sector`

### Programming bias

- Flex ratio **0.85 – 1.0** depending on the administration
- Strong ERP compliance focus — public access on part of the premises
- Varied meeting rooms (committee, delegation, workshop formats)
- Multi-purpose halls (training, town-hall style public meetings)
- Back-office tightly programmed, front-office welcoming and accessible

Sources : DGAFP _Cadres de travail dans la fonction publique_ 2023
[TO VERIFY], French _Plan de sobriété énergétique 2024_ [TO VERIFY].

### Spatial bias

- Front office with clear wayfinding + PMR-first circulations
- Back-office open-plan with some enclosed cells for sensitive files
- Dual-purpose rooms (day training / evening public meeting)

### Material & tone bias

- Palette : **institutional warm — wood + slate + soft green accents**.
  Not flashy, durable, repairable.
- Lighting 4000 K front, 3500 K back-office.
- Materials chosen for 10-year wear and easy repair.
- Minimal branding; place-names over logos.
- Furniture references : Kinnarps, Sokoa, Steelcase Flex, Bisley Lateral.

### Do not

- Over-design or "cool-office" tropes — reads as misuse of public funds.
- Materials that are delicate or expensive to repair.

## 8. Other / not in the list

If `client_profile = other`, apply **`tech_startup` defaults** as a neutral
baseline, and state this explicitly in the output : "No industry profile
selected — applying tech-startup baseline. Ask Saad to pick a specific
profile in the Brief page for finer tuning."
