# Biophilic office — implementation guide

Biophilic design fails in offices for three reasons, none of them about
research :

1. **Specification is too loose** — "add plants" becomes a sparse row of
   Ficus at reception that dies within a quarter.
2. **Maintenance is under-scoped** — no watering contract, no light-level
   survey, no replacement stock.
3. **Acoustic, HVAC and structural integration is an afterthought** —
   green walls leak, planted zones block air returns, heavy planters
   over-stress raised-floor grids.

This document is the playbook for avoiding those failures.

---

## 1. Planting density — plan envelope

### 1.1 Density tiers

Consistent with Ambius, Interface, Planteria, and Kinnarps practice
guides `[À VÉRIFIER ces sources primaires publiées]` :

| Intervention level | Plant footprint % of floor | Density heuristic      | Visible effect                         |
|--------------------|----------------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Discrete**       | 1 – 2 %                    | 1 plant / 6 – 8 m²   | Reception and café accents only          |
| **Moderate**       | 3 – 5 %                    | 1 plant / 2 – 3 m²   | Distributed, visible from every cluster  |
| **Generous**       | 5 – 8 %                    | Planted walls + distributed planters, maintenance in contract | Strong identity, measurable wellbeing   |
| **Immersive**      | 8 % +                      | Atrium gardens, mature trees, water features | Hero biophilic anchor          |

For **Lumen (2 400 m² plate)** at moderate tier : **72 – 120 m²** of
planted footprint, roughly **240 – 400 plants** if sized at medium
(≈ 0.3 m² per unit).

### 1.2 Distribution rule

Do not concentrate planting in one zone. A "moderate" tier with 5 % of
floor all in the café leaves the open-plan bare. Target : every seat
has **at least one plant in the 60° field of view** within 7 m.

### 1.3 Height stratification

- **Floor-level large planters** (Monstera, Ficus, Strelitzia) — visual
  anchor, partial screening
- **Mid-height planters** (counter height, 1.0 – 1.3 m) — separates
  zones without walls
- **Desktop small plants** — personal, variable maintenance
- **Overhead trailing planters** (Pothos, Philodendron) — biophilic
  ceiling, softens hard surfaces

Mixed stratification is what delivers Browning pattern 10 (Complexity
& Order).

## 3. Light and air requirements

### 3.1 Light (photosynthetic photon flux density, PPFD, at the leaf)

| Light environment    | PPFD target (µmol/m²/s) | Approx. office-lux equivalent `[À VÉRIFIER]` |
|----------------------|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Direct sun species   | 400 – 1 000             | Sun at window, > 10 000 lux peaks            |
| Bright indirect      | 150 – 400               | 1 500 – 5 000 lux                            |
| Medium indirect      | 50 – 150                | 500 – 1 500 lux                              |
| Low light            | 10 – 50                 | 100 – 500 lux                                |

Office ambient lighting at 500 lux (EN 12464-1, see
`design://ergonomic-workstation`) corresponds to roughly medium-
indirect light environment at the leaf — most living-plant species
will subsist but not thrive. **Supplement with plant-specific LED
growlights** for green walls or deep-plan planters.

### 3.2 Air temperature and humidity

- **Temperature** : most interior species tolerate 18 – 26 °C. Avoid
  placing plants near HVAC supply diffusers (cold jets stress tropicals).
- **Relative humidity** : 40 – 60 % is ideal for most species ; green
  walls may require local misting or a humidifier to hit 55 %+ in dry
  winter conditions.

### 3.3 Irrigation

- **Manual watering** : OK for discrete tier (< 50 plants) with a
  weekly maintenance visit
- **Sub-irrigation self-watering pots** : standard for moderate tier,
  reduces service frequency
- **Automated drip** : required for generous tier and all green walls ;
  connect to building water supply with check valves + leak detection

## 4. Maintenance — cost and cadence

### 4.1 Cost benchmarks (indicative, Paris 2024-2025)

| Intervention              | Install cost                     | Annual maintenance           |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Distributed moderate tier | 8 – 20 €/m² floor `[À VÉRIFIER]`  | 10 – 20 % of install / year  |
| Green wall (hydroponic)   | 800 – 1 800 €/m² of wall `[À VÉRIFIER]` | 20 – 30 % of install / year |
| Atrium anchor planting    | 40 – 80 €/m² floor `[À VÉRIFIER]` | 15 – 25 % / year              |
| Green roof (outside scope here) | 80 – 200 €/m² `[À VÉRIFIER]`    | 10 – 15 % / year              |

### 4.2 Maintenance contract elements

- **Weekly** (moderate and above) : watering, foliage cleaning, pest
  inspection, rotation of specimens for even light exposure
- **Monthly** : fertilisation, pruning, replacement of dead / weakened
  specimens
- **Quarterly** : deep clean, repotting if needed, substrate refresh
- **Annual** : species review, light-level survey, system diagnostic
  (green walls), KPI report to tenant

**Replacement stock** : hold **5 – 10 %** of total plant count in
reserve at the maintenance contractor's nursery.

### 4.3 Common maintenance failures

- **Overwatering** — most common killer in offices ; switch to
  sub-irrigated pots or stricter schedules
- **Air-conditioning stress** — supply diffusers blowing cold air onto
  plants causes leaf drop
- **Low-light isolation** — deep-plan planters without growlight
  supplementation decline over 6 – 12 months
- **No replacement stock** — dead plants visible for weeks become a
  negative branding signal

## 5. Natural light delivery

Every biophilic office strategy starts with **maximising natural light
delivery** (Browning pattern 6 — Dynamic & Diffuse Light).

- **Workstation orientation** : every regularly occupied seat **within
  ~7 – 8 m** of a window, **15 m absolute maximum** (Heerwagen, Kellert
  summary).
- **Glazed partitions** between open floor and enclosed rooms (focus
  rooms, meeting rooms) to borrow light. Use low-iron glass for
  colour-true transmission.
- **Skylights / atria** for deep floors ; a 300 m² atrium can condition
  a full deep-plan floor's perception of "openness".
- **Window treatment** : user-controlled mechanical blinds at each
  workstation, not central-only control. Automate high-window
  sun-tracking blinds for south facades in summer.
- **Circadian target (WELL v2 L03)** : ≥ **250 melanopic EDI lux at
  the eye** during work hours. In winter, north façades typically
  deliver < 100 melanopic EDI at 3 m from the window — fill the gap
  with tunable-white LED ceiling fixtures on a circadian schedule.

## 6. Natural materials specification

Favour (by material family, with typical install cost class) :

### 6.1 Wood

- **Solid hardwood** (oak, ash, walnut, birch) — premium tier
  (90 – 250 €/m² flooring `[À VÉRIFIER]`), high biophilic value
- **Engineered hardwood** (oak top layer, plywood core) — mid tier
  (45 – 120 €/m²), still biophilic
- **Wood-veneered MDF** — value tier, acceptable for secondary
  surfaces
- **Avoid PVC wood-effect laminate** in client-facing or high-dwell
  zones — tactile dissonance degrades biophilic effect (Browning P9
  "material connection with nature")

### 6.2 Stone

- **Marble / travertine / limestone / slate** — accent surfaces only,
  typically 200 – 600 €/m² (supply + install)
- **Terrazzo** — resilient biophilic alternative, 80 – 180 €/m²

### 6.3 Natural textiles

- **Wool (100 %)** — carpet, upholstery, acoustic panel fabric
- **Linen / flax** — upholstery, curtains
- **Leather** — seating, bar stools
- **Cotton** — curtains, cushions

Avoid uniform high-gloss synthetic surfaces — they flatten the
material vocabulary and over-reflect light.

### 6.4 Cork

Biophilic, acoustic, tactile — good for walls or ceilings in collab
and lounge zones. 30 – 80 €/m² supply.

### 6.5 Clay / concrete brut

Exposed concrete ceilings, cement plaster walls, clay tile floors —
biophilic in a minimalist, material-forward register. Budget for
acoustic mitigation : raw concrete is reflective (see §8).

## 7. Water features

Water is a strong biophilic anchor (Browning pattern 5) but introduces
real building-management issues.

### 7.1 Types appropriate to office

- **Still-water basin** (decorative, 50 – 100 L, with in-line
  filtration and UV sterilisation) — café, atrium, or reception
- **Mist wall** (ultrasonic) — adds humidity as a bonus, low flow,
  but maintenance-intensive
- **Wall-mounted sheet fountain** — visual movement without splash
- **Avoid open-surface fountains with cascading water** — splash
  zones become circulation hazards

### 7.2 Risks and requirements

- **Legionella** — any stored or slowly circulating water > 20 °C is
  a Legionella risk. Chlorinate, UV-sterilise, and schedule weekly
  inspection. Over **500 L** triggers regulatory oversight under
  French health code `[À VÉRIFIER seuils exacts]`.
- **Plumbing tap-in** — requires backflow prevention valve
- **Humidity management** — a 1 m² mist wall can add 0.5 – 1.0 L/hr
  to the building's moisture load. Check HVAC capacity.
- **Maintenance** — weekly clean, biweekly media change (UV lamp,
  filter) at a minimum.

Budget a single still-water feature in the café or atrium, not
scattered installations.

## 8. Acoustic interaction

Biophilic planting contributes to acoustic absorption — approximately
**0.30 – 0.70 m² Sabine per m² of densely planted area** `[À VÉRIFIER
par espèce, mesurée en bande]`. Not a substitute for a Class A
ceiling but a meaningful supplement.

- Planted walls : **10 – 40 % absorption** depending on media, plant
  density, and frequency
- Tall potted planters : act as low-frequency diffusers more than
  absorbers, help break standing waves

See `design://acoustic-standards §5`.

## 9. Fractal patterns and biomorphic forms (Browning pattern 8 & 10)

Patterns with a **mid-range fractal dimension D ≈ 1.3 – 1.5** trigger
measurable stress reduction (Taylor et al., see
`design://neuroarchitecture §7.1`). Applied to office interiors :

- **Acoustic panels** — many commercial ranges use perforation
  patterns at D ≈ 1.3 – 1.5 (Baswa, Plaforama, BuzziSpace)
- **Carpet tiles** — Interface Biophilic series, Milliken Biophilia
  (fractal, non-grid layouts)
- **Wayfinding signage** — organic, non-grid curves
- **Ceiling baffles** — suspended at varying heights forming an
  irregular "canopy" rather than a grid

**Avoid** strict grids and monochrome uniform surfaces in dwell zones
(café, lounge, event) — they feel mechanical even when well-
executed.

## 10. Thermal and airflow variability (Browning pattern 4)

Perfectly uniform temperature + zero airflow = boring and fatiguing
(neuroarchitecture view : lack of stimulation degrades alertness).

- **Operable windows where the façade permits** — at least one in
  every zone over 100 m², even if only seasonally useful
- **Occupant controls** (thermostat, fan speed) within 3 m of every
  work area
- **Ceiling fans** in café, lounge, and high-ceiling volumes —
  subtle airflow plus ceiling visual interest
- **Seasonal planting rotations** to mark the year

## 11. Prospect–Refuge design moves

See `design://neuroarchitecture §6` for theory. In biophilic terms :

- **Prospect anchor** : a long sight-line across the floor, ideally
  to a biophilic element (planted wall, atrium, window with greenery
  beyond). Minimum 15 m ; ideal 25 – 40 m.
- **Refuge pockets** : enclosed or partially enclosed zones with
  natural materials (wood, upholstered fabric) and some planting. One
  refuge pocket per 50 – 80 occupants (same density as ART restorative
  zones).
- **Transition zones** between prospect and refuge soften the shift
  — a canopy of trailing plants, a partial partition, a change of
  ceiling height.

## 12. Measurement and KPIs — post-occupancy

Track quarterly for the first year, then annually :

| KPI                                           | Target (Lumen scale)    |
|-----------------------------------------------|-------------------------|
| % of seats with direct window sight-line      | ≥ 90 %                  |
| Plant count density (plants / 100 m²)         | ≥ 30 (moderate tier)    |
| Employee-reported "connection with nature"    | ≥ 4 / 5 on custom survey, or +10 points Leesman-equivalent |
| Browning patterns expressed                   | ≥ 8 / 14 for "very good" tier `[À VÉRIFIER origine target 8/14]` |
| Plant attrition rate (deaths per quarter)     | ≤ 5 %                   |
| Green-wall survival rate                      | ≥ 90 % after 12 months  |
| Melanopic EDI lux at 3 m from window (winter noon) | ≥ 200 lux (bias toward biophilic comfort, not circadian floor) `[À VÉRIFIER]` |

## 13. Worked example — Lumen biophilic specification

For the Lumen brief at moderate tier :

- **Reception + café (south façade, 300 m²)** : mature planted wall
  (Monstera, Ficus lyrata, Philodendron), floor planters with
  Schefflera, table centrepieces in ceramic ; direct-sun species
- **Open-plan devs (north façade, ~800 m²)** : distributed mid-height
  planters (Dracaena, Pothos, ZZ plant) every 10 – 12 m ; desk-level
  small plants optional ; no green wall (low light)
- **Atrium around central stair (~50 m²)** : skylight + hero tree
  (Ficus lyrata, 3 m) + trailing Pothos overhead + carpet in walkable
  area
- **Meeting rooms** : small plant on credenza, visible from seated
  position ; door always partly glazed to borrow plant views
- **Town hall** : seasonal large planters that can be moved for
  event reconfiguration (wheels, self-watering)

**Count check** : ~2 400 m² × 4 % moderate tier = ~96 m² planted
footprint ≈ **320 plants** at 0.3 m² per plant average.

**Budget** : 10 – 18 €/m² × 2 400 m² = **24 000 – 43 000 € install**,
**3 500 – 8 000 €/year maintenance** for the moderate tier. Green wall
in atrium adds ~8 000 – 18 000 € install + 2 000 – 5 000 €/year
maintenance.
