# Collaboration spaces

Leesman 2024 : **52 %** of employees cite "informal, unplanned meetings" as
important to their role, and **73 %** say the office supports idea-sharing.
This surface is the single biggest post-COVID differentiator vs the home
office — get it right.

## 1. Phone booth — 1 person

- **External footprint** : 1.0 – 1.5 m² (e.g. Framery One Compact ~1.03 m²
  external)
- **Required clearance** : ~1.85 m back-to-front including door swing
  (Framery One Compact)
- **Interior** : integrated table 400 × 750 mm at fixed height, adjustable
  stool 590 – 850 mm
- **Typical use** : 15 – 45 min calls, 1 :1 sensitive conversations, focus
- **Planning density** : **6 – 10 per 100 FTE** (higher in hybrid / flex)
- **Acoustic** : manufacturer advertises ~30 dB reduction ; do not rely
  only on that — locate away from corridors and noise sources

Key products : Framery One / One Compact, Haworth Work, Orangebox Air3,
Steelcase Work Pods.

## 2. Huddle room — 2 to 4 persons

- **Internal footprint** : **6 – 10 m²** (e.g. 3.0 × 2.5 m)
- **Furniture** : round or oval table 1 000 – 1 200 mm diameter, 4 chairs,
  wall-mounted screen (55" – 65"), whiteboard or glass wall
- **Planning density** : **3 – 5 per 100 FTE**
- **Typical use** : sprint reviews, 1 :1 + 1, quick design decisions,
  standups

Door 900 mm clear, single leaf. Glazed facade improves inclusion and is a
biophilic asset (visual connection).

## 3. Medium meeting room — 6 to 8 persons

- **Internal footprint** : **18 – 25 m²** (e.g. 5.0 × 4.0 m)
- **Furniture** : rectangular table 2 400 × 1 200 mm, 6 – 8 chairs, 75" – 86"
  screen, interactive whiteboard
- **Planning density** : **2 – 4 per 100 FTE**
- **AV baseline** : camera + mic + speaker bar (Logitech Rally Bar, Poly
  Studio, Neat Bar), HDMI table connector, wireless screen sharing
- **Acoustic** : DnT,A 35 – 40 dB vs corridor, TR60 ≤ 0.5 s (Performant
  level per NF S 31-080), background noise ≤ 35 dB(A)

## 4. Large meeting / boardroom — 10 to 14 persons

- **Internal footprint** : **30 – 45 m²**
- **Furniture** : boardroom table 4 000 – 5 000 × 1 400 – 1 600 mm, 12 – 14
  executive chairs, dual-screen or projector, credenza
- **Planning density** : **1 – 2 per 100 FTE**
- **Use cases** : steering committees, client pitches, board meetings,
  formal workshops
- **Enhancements** : premium materials (wood, marble accents), art wall,
  dedicated VC system with 4K camera, dimming + circadian light

## 5. Town hall / event space — 40+ persons

- **Footprint** : **80 – 150 m²** for 40 – 80 seats in theatre layout,
  **150 – 300 m²** for 100 – 150 seats
- **Configurable** : stackable chairs (Vitra Tip Ton, Magis Jam), flat or
  stepped platform, retractable screen
- **AV** : PA system, stage mic, recording + streaming, accent lighting
- **Use cases** : all-hands, lunch-and-learns, product launches, external
  events
- **Programming note** : duplicates the function of the café if big enough
  → consider combining café-by-day / town hall-by-event (common pattern)

## 6. Project / war room — 4 to 10 persons, persistent

- **Footprint** : **15 – 25 m²**
- **Distinct from** a meeting room because teams **leave material up** —
  pinboards, whiteboards across 2 – 3 walls, plotter output on wall, models
- **Best served by** : neighbourhood adjacency to the dedicated team's
  desks, so members can roll in and out of focus/project context

## 7. Informal collab — couches, high tables

- **Footprint** : **4 – 8 m² per island**
- **Furniture** : 2 – 4 seat sofa with integrated table, booth seating,
  high bar + 4 stools
- **Planning density** : **1 island per 10 FTE** in collab-heavy org
- **Use cases** : coffee chat → real decision, onboarding, 1 :1 with
  junior, lightweight cross-team

The Leesman "informal unplanned meetings" score rises most reliably when
these islands are **embedded inside team neighbourhoods**, not banked into
a single breakout zone.

## 8. Focus rooms — 1 to 2 persons, enclosed

- **Footprint** : **3 – 6 m²**
- Between a phone booth and a huddle — big enough to sit properly at a
  desk for 2 – 4 hours of head-down work
- **Planning density** : **2 – 4 per 100 FTE**
- **Interior spec** : desk 1 400 × 800 mm, 1 task chair, acoustic ceiling +
  absorbent panel wall, dimmable task light + ambient

## 9. Video-first rooms

Post-2020, a growing share of meetings has remote participants. Every
enclosed meeting room should default to "equal voice" design :

- Camera positioned so that all in-room participants are clearly visible
- Table depth aligned with camera field of view (typically 1 200 mm)
- Acoustic treatment to avoid the "cave echo" picked up by far-end
- Lighting from the front, not behind participants

## 10. Adjacency rules

- Meeting rooms **clustered near neighbourhood teams**, not banked in a
  meeting-room-only corridor
- Phone booths **distributed every 20 – 30 m** of open floor so users never
  walk further than 15 m
- Town hall / event near the café (shared circulation, deliveries,
  operations)
- Board and premium meeting rooms near **reception and a dedicated loo
  block** if clients cycle through

## 11. Booking and utilisation

Industry utilisation rates for meeting rooms (pre-booking, 2023-2024) :

- **Sub-scale** small rooms (≤ 4 pers) : **65 – 80 %** booked, **50 – 65 %**
  actually used (ghost meetings)
- **Boardrooms** : typically **30 – 45 %** booked — under-utilised, which
  is normal
- Target : a utilisation > 70 % in actual use → redesign or add rooms
